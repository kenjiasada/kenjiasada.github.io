Tragedy of shoplifting by elderly people

The rate of elderly people in the arrests of shoplifting is increasing. What's the reason of this tragedy. Is there any corelation between the increase of the old people and crime rate.

By KENJI ASADA

Published July 28, 2019

In 2016, 38% of the arrested for shoplifting were older than 65 years old. Shoplifting has been redarded as the crime of young age, but that formula is no longer able to apply. According to the article by AERA, they commit shoplifting not only because of poverty, but also because of isolation. Under the decrease of population, more and ore oled people lives in single and sometimes have weak connection with the community or neighbors, and some of them feel an urge to commit shoplifting.

This is still a hypothesis and just one possible reason of the tragedy. The relation between the aging society and crime has not been proven yet. Here I want to let you show some analysis by data.

Coefficient between the rate of aging population and shoplifting

To see the coefficient, I used a linear regression on it. Before I run the regression code, I had omitted exceptional area previously -- 1) the areas the average income is over 7 million yen, and 2) the areas where no shoplifting crime was detected. 1) is in order to avoid to calculate the area where the land price is high -- i.e. those areas are usually commercial areas. 2) is for guaranteeing the accuracy of the calculation.

According to the result, the coefficient is 0.0172. This means that the shoplifting rate will increase 0.0172 %point if the aging rate increase 1 %point. But the p-value of the coefficient is 0.132. So the credibility of the calculation is not enough.

I would say that it's difficult to make it more accurate because the number of municipality is too small. Unfortunately, Japanese police system is splitted by prefectures and their statistics are different each other. We can get shoplifting data from the website of Tokyo police office, but cannot from the police of their neighbors...
